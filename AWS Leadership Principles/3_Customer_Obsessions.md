# Story - Accesibility Enhancements in Invoices/Recipts/Credit Notes etc

## Situation: 
>An accessibility audit flagged that Telstra's customer invoice templates failed accessibility standards — customers using screen readers or other assistive technology couldn't reliably navigate or understand their own bills. For those customers, something as basic as knowing what they owed and why required help from someone else, or wasn't possible at all.

## Task: 
>Around the same time, a rebranding initiative was underway to refresh the invoice templates with new logos, fonts, and colors — a purely visual scope. Nobody had connected the two: the rebrand wasn't going to fix the accessibility problem, and the accessibility problem had no dedicated project or timeline of its own. I didn't want a customer's ability to read their own bill to depend on a separate initiative getting prioritized someday.

## Action: 
>I dug into the accessibility audit and the underlying issues myself — reviewing how screen readers interpret document structure, contrast, and layout — rather than just taking the audit's findings at face value. That let me understand exactly what a truly accessible template needed, not just a visual one. I then pushed to fold accessibility into the rebrand rather than let the two stay separate, working with the product owner to get it explicitly added to the backlog. Working from that deeper understanding, I partnered with the UI/UX designer to make sure the new template was designed accessibility-first — proper structure, contrast, and layout that would actually work for screen readers — rather than a visual refresh that would need to be reworked again later. I then worked with the accessibility and development teams to implement and validate it against accessibility standards before rollout, so the fix shipped with the rebrand instead of trailing behind it.

## Result: 
>The redesigned invoice achieved a Triple-A accessibility rating, the highest standard. Because it became the new default template rather than an opt-in accommodation, every one of Telstra's roughly five million monthly bills, receipts, and notices now goes out in an accessible format — so any customer who relies on assistive technology like a screen reader gets a usable bill automatically, without needing to request one. What started as an audit finding became the permanent standard for every invoice going forward.
